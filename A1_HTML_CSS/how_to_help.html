How to Help

Be Curious

It is important that we all know how our rubbish ends up in the ocean, and how long it hangs around for. This can help us make informed decisions on what types of materials we choose to include in our daily lives and methods of disposal.

Here is a list of common items used by humans that are found in the sea, and their lifetimes:



Type of rubbish Time to break down
apple core 1 month
cardboard box 2 months
balloon 4 years
plastic bag 20 years
crisp packet 80 years
drinks can 200 years
plastic bottle 450 years
fishing line 600 years
glass bottle 1 million years

Join us for a beach clean up

By the end of 2022 we aim to have hosted a beach cleanup in every coastal county at least once - an ambitious target given our resources with which you can help directly.

For the month of October we are on the East coast. All of our cleanup sessions run from 10:00-14:00.

Equipment will be provided so just bring your warm clothes!

See upcoming events below:

  • October 12th - Skerries Harbour
  • October 16th - Laytown Beach

Any updates to the list above will be detailed on this site and on our social media accounts.

Donations

As a non-profit, non-government funded organisation, we rely on the generosity of the Irish community to carry out the work we do.

Donations can be sent via our Just Giving page. As always, sharing to your own network is the most effective way to get the word around.
